# Predicting Well Performance and Failure Risk in Alberta Oil & Gas Wells

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Alberta oil and gas well data to identify patterns in production lifespan, operational decline, and failure risk. Using Python, SQL, and Power BI, the project develops a decision-support framework that helps identify high-performing wells, detect early warning signals, and support long-term production planning.

The solution combines production metrics, well attributes, and integrity-related indicators to evaluate long-term well performance and classify wells into meaningful risk and performance categories.

---

## Business Problem

Oil and gas operators often lack early visibility into which wells will perform efficiently over time. This creates challenges in:

- Capital allocation
- Production forecasting
- Operational planning
- Risk management
- Maintenance prioritization

Some wells maintain strong long-term performance, while others experience rapid production decline or operational failure. This project uses historical Alberta well data to support more proactive and data-driven decision-making.

---

## Objectives

This project addresses the following business questions:

1. What well attributes are associated with long-lasting wells?
2. What is the average productive duration across well types and regions?
3. Which wells show early signs of decline or operational risk?
4. Can wells be classified into long-lasting, average-performing, and early-failure groups?
5. How can production and integrity indicators improve long-term forecasting?

## Key Insights

- Early production behavior strongly influences long-term well performance
- Wells can be segmented into long-lasting, average-performing, and early-failure categories
- Well type, fluid type, and depth significantly impact productive lifespan
- Integrating production and integrity indicators improves operational risk identification
- Early warning signals help support proactive decision-making
